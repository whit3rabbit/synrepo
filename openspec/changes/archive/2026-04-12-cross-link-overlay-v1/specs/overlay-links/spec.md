## MODIFIED Requirements

### Requirement: Define evidence-verified cross-link candidates
synrepo SHALL define a cross-link candidate as a proposed relationship between two graph nodes that is generated by the synthesis pipeline via a two-stage triage (cheap deterministic prefilter followed by LLM-backed evidence extraction), verified against cited evidence through fuzzy LCS matching, scored for confidence, and stored in the overlay as non-authoritative until a human declaration promotes it to the graph. Cross-links never enter the graph directly from the pipeline. The prefilter SHALL use deterministic signals only (name/identifier match, graph distance cutoff) so candidate generation cost is bounded by repository size, not by LLM throughput.

#### Scenario: Generate a candidate cross-link
- **WHEN** the synthesis pipeline infers a possible relationship between a code node and a prose node with cited evidence
- **THEN** the overlay stores the candidate with: source nodes, inferred relationship kind, confidence score and tier, cited evidence spans with LCS ratios, both endpoints' content hashes at generation time, and full provenance
- **AND** the stored candidate is labeled non-authoritative and does not modify the graph

#### Scenario: Reject a candidate with no verifiable evidence
- **WHEN** the synthesis pipeline proposes a cross-link but cannot produce cited evidence that verifies above the minimum LCS threshold
- **THEN** the overlay contract requires the candidate to be discarded or held at `below_threshold`
- **AND** the response does not surface an unverified candidate as probable

#### Scenario: Prefilter discards infeasible candidate pairs
- **WHEN** the synthesis pipeline considers a (prose, code) pair whose name-match score is zero and graph distance exceeds the configured cutoff
- **THEN** the prefilter discards the pair without invoking the LLM
- **AND** no candidate row is written for the discarded pair

### Requirement: Define confidence scoring for cross-link candidates
synrepo SHALL define a confidence scoring model for cross-link candidates that ranks them by the strength and quality of their cited evidence. The model combines span count, per-span LCS ratio, average span length, and graph distance into a numeric score, and maps the score into one of three surfaced tiers: `high` (surfaced in agent-facing card responses at Deep budget), `review_queue` (visible through `synrepo links review` and `synrepo findings` but not in default card responses), and `below_threshold` (withheld from card responses and review queue; visible in `synrepo findings` audit output only). The numeric score SHALL be persisted for audit and threshold tuning; only the tier SHALL be exposed in card responses.

#### Scenario: Surface a high-tier candidate to an agent
- **WHEN** an agent requests cross-link context at Deep budget and a `high`-tier candidate exists with fresh provenance
- **THEN** the response includes the candidate under its overlay source-store label with its confidence tier
- **AND** the candidate is not presented as a graph-backed fact

#### Scenario: Withhold a below-threshold candidate from card responses
- **WHEN** a cross-link candidate falls into the `below_threshold` tier
- **THEN** the card response omits the candidate and does not imply its existence
- **AND** the candidate remains queryable through `synrepo findings` for operator audit

#### Scenario: Numeric score is not exposed in agent responses
- **WHEN** an agent receives a card response containing a surfaced cross-link
- **THEN** the response includes the tier label (`high` or `review_queue`) without the underlying float score
- **AND** the float score is retrievable only through explicit audit queries

### Requirement: Define cross-link review and promotion workflow
synrepo SHALL define a review workflow for proposed cross-links, available in `curated` mode only. The workflow SHALL include a human-review queue exposed through `synrepo links review` listing `review_queue`-tier candidates, a promotion path that writes an accepted candidate to the graph as an edge with `Epistemic::HumanDeclared` provenance, and a rejection path that marks the candidate rejected without touching the graph. Promoted links SHALL NOT carry overlay epistemic labels in the graph; the type boundary between `OverlayEpistemic` and `graph::Epistemic` SHALL be enforced at the type system level. `auto` mode SHALL NOT expose a promotion workflow; auto-mode users still receive `high`-tier candidates in card responses but cannot promote them.

#### Scenario: Promote a reviewed cross-link to the graph in curated mode
- **WHEN** a human reviewer accepts a proposed cross-link through `synrepo links accept <id>` in curated mode
- **THEN** the accepted link is written to the graph as a human-declared edge with `Epistemic::HumanDeclared`, reviewer identity recorded in the graph edge's provenance, and the overlay candidate row updated with `promoted_at` timestamp and a `graph_edge_id` back-reference
- **AND** an audit row recording the promotion event is appended to `cross_link_audit`

#### Scenario: Reject a proposed cross-link
- **WHEN** a human reviewer rejects a proposed cross-link through `synrepo links reject <id>`
- **THEN** the overlay records the rejection with reviewer identity and timestamp in `cross_link_audit`
- **AND** the candidate row is updated to `rejected` state
- **AND** the rejected candidate is excluded from future surfacing unless explicitly re-evaluated

#### Scenario: Auto mode cannot promote
- **WHEN** `synrepo links accept` is invoked in `auto` mode
- **THEN** the command fails with an explicit error that promotion is curated-mode-only
- **AND** no graph write is attempted

### Requirement: Define cross-link audit trail
synrepo SHALL maintain an audit trail for all cross-link candidates, including generation provenance, confidence score history, freshness state transitions, review decisions, promotion or rejection outcomes, and prune events. The audit trail SHALL be queryable and SHALL survive candidate deletion. Audit records SHALL NOT be deleted or mutated after write.

#### Scenario: Audit a promoted cross-link
- **WHEN** an operator queries the audit trail for a link that was promoted to the graph
- **THEN** synrepo returns the original candidate provenance, confidence score, reviewer decision, and promotion timestamp
- **AND** the audit record is independent of whether the overlay candidate entry still exists

#### Scenario: Audit survives candidate prune
- **WHEN** a candidate is pruned from `cross_links` because an endpoint was deleted
- **THEN** the audit rows for that candidate remain queryable
- **AND** subsequent audit queries can reconstruct the full lifecycle including the prune event
